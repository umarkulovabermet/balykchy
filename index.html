About us balykchy

In the coastal zone of Lake Issyk-Kul, 175 km. from Bishkek, there is the city of
Balykchy, which got its name from the Kyrgyz word "fisherman". In Soviet
times, it was known as Rybachye, but after Kyrgyzstan gained
independence in 1993, it received the name Balykchi.
Before the collapse of the Soviet Union, the city had the status of the center
of the largest Issyk-Kul region and united rail, road and water transport into
a single junction. Thanks to the transit routes of Balykchy, Kyrgyzstan is
connected with China. The roads, bending around the lake from the north
and south, cross the city in the directions of Karakol and Naryn.
The population of Balykchy, according to the data of 2009, totals 42.4
thousand inhabitants.
